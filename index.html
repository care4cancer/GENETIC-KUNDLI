How Your Genetic Kundli Is Created.

Decoding your DNA in 4 Seamless Steps.

1

Sample & Logistics

Saliva (2 ml) / EDTA blood (3–5 ml). Smart tracking, cold‑chain where required, consent + TRF. We provide easy-to-use collection kits for at-home or clinic-based sampling. Samples are securely transported to our partner labs with full chain-of-custody documentation to ensure integrity and privacy throughout the process.

2

Sequencing & QC

Targeted panels (hybrid‑capture/amplicon) or exome; uniformity ≥90%, on‑target ≥70%. Using state-of-the-art NGS platforms like Illumina, we ensure high-quality data generation with rigorous quality checks at every step to minimize errors and achieve reliable sequencing results.

3

Bioinformatics

BWA/GATK‑class pipeline, CNV/SV support, HGVS nomenclature, transcript selection best‑practice. Our automated yet expert-reviewed pipeline processes raw data into meaningful variants, incorporating the latest databases like ClinVar and gnomAD for accurate annotation and classification.

4

Reporting

Tiered variants, actionability notes, therapy/clinical trial pointers (informational), and re‑analysis option. The final Genetic Kundli report is delivered digitally in PDF format, with clear visualizations, evidence-based references, and actionable insights to empower clinicians in making informed, personalized care decisions.

Comprehensive Disease Panels

Indicative catalogue across oncology, cardiogenetics, neurogenetics, rare disease, pharmacogenomics, and reproductive health. Panels evolve with guidelines; custom panels available.

Oncology – Solid Tumour Panels

Site‑specific and pan‑cancer options for therapy selection & hereditary risk.

  • Breast/Ovarian: BRCA1, BRCA2, PALB2, CHEK2, ATM, PTEN, TP53, CDH1, RAD51C/D.
  • Lung (NSCLC): EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF, KRAS (incl. G12C), MET exon14, RET, NTRK1‑3, HER2/ERBB2.
  • Colorectal: KRAS/NRAS, BRAF, MSI/dMMR (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2), APC, PIK3CA.
  • Prostate: BRCA1/2, ATM, CHEK2, HOXB13, PTEN, MSH2, CDK12.
  • Melanoma: BRAF, NRAS, KIT, NF1.
  • Glioma: IDH1/2, 1p/19q codeletion (CNV), TERT, ATRX, TP53.
  • Pan‑Cancer (50–500 genes): Actionable SNVs/indels, CNVs, fusions.

Oncology – Hematologic

Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasms for diagnosis, prognosis, minimal residual disease (MRD) support.

  • AML/MDS: FLT3, NPM1, DNMT3A, IDH1/2, RUNX1, ASXL1, TP53, TET2, SF3B1, SRSF2.
  • MPN: JAK2, CALR, MPL, CSF3R.
  • CLL: TP53, NOTCH1, SF3B1, BIRC3, MYD88; IGHV status (separate assay).
  • ALL: IKZF1, CRLF2, JAK1/2, ABL1/2; key fusions (ETV6‑RUNX1, BCR‑ABL1).
  • Lymphoma: MYD88, CD79B, EZH2, CARD11, BCL2/6 rearrangements.

Hereditary Cancer Predisposition

Germline risk assessment panels.

  • Breast/Ovarian: BRCA1/2, PALB2, CHEK2, ATM, PTEN, TP53, RAD51C/D, BRIP1.
  • Lynch Syndrome: MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM.
  • Polyposis: APC, MUTYH (biallelic), STK11, SMAD4, BMPR1A, POLE/POLD1.
  • Endocrine: RET (MEN2), MEN1, VHL, SDHx (SDHB, SDHC, SDHD).
  • Li‑Fraumeni: TP53 comprehensive.

Diabetes Panel

Covers common polygenic risk for T2D and rare monogenic forms.

  • Type 1 Diabetes (T1D): HLA-DRB1, HLA-DQB1, PTPN22, CTLA4
  • Type 2 Diabetes (T2D): TCF7L2, FTO, KCNJ11, ABCC8, PPARG
  • MODY: GCK, HNF1A, HNF4A, HNF1B
  • Neonatal Diabetes: KCNJ11, ABCC8, INS

Hypertension Panel

Risk assessment for essential and monogenic hypertension.

  • Essential Hypertension: ACE, AGT, AGTR1, NOS3
  • Liddle Syndrome: SCNN1A, SCNN1B, SCNN1G
  • Glucocorticoid-Remediable Aldosteronism: CYP11B1, CYP11B2
  • Familial Hyperaldosteronism: KCNJ5

Cardiogenetics

Inherited cardiomyopathy, arrhythmia, and aortopathy.

  • HCM/DCM/ARVC: MYH7, MYBPC3, TTN, DSP, PKP2, LMNA, TNNI3, TNNT2.
  • Channelopathies: KCNQ1, KCNH2, SCN5A (LQTS, Brugada).
  • Aortopathy: FBN1 (Marfan), TGFBR1/2, SMAD3, ACTA2.

Neurogenetics

Epilepsy, neuromuscular, and neurodegenerative disorders.

  • Epilepsy: SCN1A, SCN2A, KCNQ2/3, CDKL5, PCDH19, STXBP1.
  • Neuromuscular: DMD, SMN1/2 (copy number), RYR1, COL6A1‑3.
  • Ataxia/Leukodystrophy: FXN (FRDA), ATXN1/2/3, ABCD1, EIF2B genes.
  • Neurodegeneration (selected): APP, PSEN1/2, MAPT, GRN, LRRK2, GBA.

Reproductive & Prenatal

Carrier screening, prenatal (diagnostic), and NIPT*.

  • Expanded Carrier Screen: CFTR, HBB/HBA (thalassemia), SMN1 (SMA), GJB2, PAH, G6PD and more (100–500 genes).
  • Prenatal Diagnostic (invasive): Aneuploidy + microdeletions (CMA) and targeted single‑gene panels.
  • NIPT*: Trisomy 21/18/13; sex chromosome aneuploidy (lab‑policy dependent). *Screening, not diagnostic.

Rare Disease – Exome/Genome

Undiagnosed disorders, developmental delay, metabolic disease.

  • Clinical Exome (solo/duo/trio): SNV/indel + clinically significant CNV.
  • Genome (selected): SVs, non‑coding hotspots; mitochondrial add‑on.
  • Mitochondrial: Full mtDNA with heteroplasmy estimation.

Pharmacogenomics (PGx)

Medication response & safety (CPIC‑aligned reporting).

  • CYP2D6, CYP2C19, CYP2C9, VKORC1 (psych, PPI, anticoagulants).
  • SLCO1B1 (statin myopathy risk).
  • DPYD, UGT1A1 (oncology dosing safety).
  • HLA‑B*15:02 (carbamazepine), HLA‑B*57:01 (abacavir).

Liquid Biopsy & MRD (Oncology)

ctDNA for actionable variants, tumour dynamics, and MSI/TMB (lab‑dependent).

  • Actionable hotspot panel (20–100 genes).
  • Personalised MRD (tumour‑informed) where applicable.

This catalogue is representative and updated as guidelines evolve (ACMG/AMP, NCCN/ESMO, CPIC). Gene lists may be tailored to clinical context and local regulations.

Your Genetic Kundli® Report

A concise, clinician‑friendly dossier summarising key variants, inheritance, pharmacogenomic insights, and evidence strength—mapped to your indication.

  • Highlights: Pathogenic/Likely Pathogenic variants, actionable findings, carrier status (if applicable).
  • Therapy Pointers: Guideline references (informational), clinical trial look‑ups.*
  • PGx Card: Key gene–drug pairs and dosing guidance references.
  • Re‑analysis: Optional periodic reinterpretation as evidence evolves.

*We provide informational pointers; treatment decisions rest with the treating clinician.
